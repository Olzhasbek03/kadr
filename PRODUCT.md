# Kormem

## What it is

A shared disposable camera for weddings and celebrations in Kazakhstan, web-only. The host creates an event and prints a QR code; guests open a link on their phone, get a limited roll of shots on an in-browser camera, and nothing is visible until the reveal moment the host sets. Then the whole night opens in one shared gallery.

The name: kazakh «көрме» is both "exhibition" and the imperative "don't look". The product is exactly that pun: don't look tonight, the exhibition opens in the morning.

## Register

Split. The marketing surface (`/`) is **brand**: image-led, emotional, the design is the pitch. The app surfaces (`/dashboard/*`, `/e/*`, `/login`) are **product**: quiet, fast, trustworthy tools that inherit the brand's palette and type but drop the theatrics.

## Users

- **Hosts**: engaged couples and their families (often the bride or a younger sibling organizing for parents), plus event agencies and tamada. Design-aware Instagram natives. They pay.
- **Guests**: everyone at the той, from teenagers to grandparents on budget Android phones. No account, no app, three taps to the camera. They must never feel friction.

## Brand personality

Tender, ceremonial, in motion. The visual world comes from real wedding photography: hazy golden-hour light, ivory tulle, motion blur, the deep crimson of the сырға салу scarf, near-black suits, misty steppe fields. The беташар ceremony (the bride's face veiled, then revealed) is the product's own story told in tradition: hidden first, then shown.

Voice: warm and plain. No startup verbs, no exclamation marks doing the work of feeling. Kazakh and Russian first, English third.

## References

- **once.film is the explicit role model** (owner decision, 2026-07): its editorial serif titles, the persistent try-me QR, the invitation-card designer, the "camera is ready" notification, and the app-playing-itself hero are the bar to meet and beat. Kormem stays web-first where Once is app-first: that is the differentiator, not a compromise.
- Still not POV's playful gadget energy, not Scene's dark-app slickness.
- No darkroom/film-noir aesthetic as the site default. The photos are light; the site is light. (Dark surfaces live in the camera tool and the noir invite template.)
- No AI-landing grammar: no uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, no three equal feature cards, no glassmorphism-by-default.

## Strategic design principles

1. **The photograph is the interface.** Marketing sections lean on full-bleed photography; UI recedes to ivory, ink, and one crimson.
2. **The reveal is the product.** Every surface that can echo hidden-then-shown (veil scrims, develop-in transitions, countdowns) should, quietly.
3. **A guest on a cheap phone outranks a judge on a MacBook.** Camera and gallery stay fast, legible, thumb-first.
4. **One accent.** Deep ceremonial crimson `oklch(0.41 0.125 8)`. Everything else is ivory, warm near-black, and photography.
